Welcome, and thank you for visiting!

My research focuses on three domains: equity crowdfunding (ECF), decentralized finance, and artificial intelligence (AI).

First, I examine ECF as a distinct entrepreneurial finance setting characterized by heterogenous investors, due diligence, and platform-mediated interactions.
I study how information is produced, transmitted, processed and aggregated in these environments, and how these processes shape capital allocation, investor behavior, and entrepreneurial venture outcomes.

Second, I analyze decentralized markets and organizations, particularly those enabled by blockchain technologies (such as DAOs).
This stream investigates mechanisms of value creation in the absence of centralized intermediaries, with emphasis on governance design, incentive alignment, and the efficiency of decentralized coordination.

Third, I study the role of AI in capital markets.
I focus on how AI alters information production, screening, and decision-making processes, and how its deployment affects capital market efficiency, strategic interaction, and organizational boundaries.

Across these domains, my research is unified by a common interest in how technological and institutional innovations reshape information environments and, in turn, influence economic behavior and market outcomes.
